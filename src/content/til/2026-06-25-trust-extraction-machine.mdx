The influencer marketing industry runs on **digital Taylorism** — breaking human influence into measurable, optimizable units. Agencies use sentiment analysis, look-alike creator finders, and fatigue predictors to treat creators like stocks. The substitutes replacing them are what Baudrillard called **simulacra**: the evolution from organic review → paid influencer → UGC actor → AI persona ends at a hyperreal stage where there's no original experience being referenced at all — just an optimized shell that refers to itself.

The mechanism keeping it working is **parasocial persuasion**. Research in *Acta Psychologica* found that once a viewer develops enough social attraction to a creator, the brain stops running calculative checks for commercial motive — manufactured parasocial bonds are a measurable persuasive tool, not a side effect.

The cumulative cost is **reality check fatigue** — the constant background labor of verifying whether what you're seeing is real. The logical endpoint is dead internet theory: a closed loop of AI personas and bot engagement that no longer requires authentic human participation, only the appearance of it.
